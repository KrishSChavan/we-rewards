This Founding Vendor Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of the Agreement Date above by and between Krish Chavan, operating as WeRewards ("Company"), and the Vendor identified above ("Vendor"). Together, the parties are the "Parties."

This Agreement governs Vendor's access to and use of the WeRewards platform during the Founding Period and, upon conversion, on a paid subscription basis thereafter.

1. Definitions

  • "Platform" means the WeRewards software application, including the vendor terminal, the student-facing app, the administrative dashboard, and all associated infrastructure.
  • "Terminal Device" or "Device" means the electronic device provided by Company to Vendor for use with the Platform's vendor terminal backend.
  • "Founding Period" means the period from the Agreement Date through September 30, 2026 (inclusive).
  • "Subscription Period" means each calendar month beginning on or after October 1, 2026 during which this Agreement is in effect.
  • "Founding Monthly Fee" means the discounted monthly subscription rate set forth in the Key Terms above, locked in for Vendor as a founding participant.
  • "Points" means the loyalty points awarded to students through the Platform in connection with qualifying purchases at Vendor's location.

2. Term

This Agreement begins on the Agreement Date and continues until terminated by either Party in accordance with Section 12 (Termination). The Agreement does not expire automatically at the end of the Founding Period; it converts to a paid subscription as described in Section 5.

3. Platform Services

Subject to this Agreement, Company grants Vendor a limited, non-exclusive, non-transferable license to use the WeRewards Platform for the duration of this Agreement to:

  • Award loyalty points to students by entering student earn codes into the vendor terminal;
  • Manage and publish your business's reward offerings through the terminal's ITEMS interface;
  • Accept student point redemptions at your location through the terminal's REDEEM interface;
  • View transaction history, customer engagement analytics, and revenue summaries through the terminal's STATS interface;
  • Configure your business profile (points-per-dollar rate, quick-amount buttons, address, logo) through the terminal's SETTINGS interface.

Company reserves the right to modify Platform features at any time. Material reductions in core functionality will be communicated to Vendor with reasonable advance notice.

4. Equipment Loan

4.1 Loan of Device

Company will provide Vendor with one (1) Terminal Device (described as an electronic device connected to the WeRewards terminal backend). The Device is loaned to Vendor at no charge during the Founding Period and at no additional hardware fee thereafter. The Device remains the sole property of Company at all times. This arrangement constitutes a bailment, not a sale or lease.

4.2 Vendor Responsibilities

Vendor agrees to:

  • Use the Device solely for operating the WeRewards vendor terminal at the Vendor's listed business location;
  • Keep the Device in good working condition, free from physical damage caused by misuse or negligence;
  • Maintain a stable internet connection at the business location sufficient to operate the terminal;
  • Not install unauthorized software on the Device, modify the Device's firmware, or attempt to access systems beyond the WeRewards terminal;
  • Promptly notify Company if the Device is lost, stolen, or damaged;
  • Not transfer, sublease, or make the Device available to any third party without Company's prior written consent.

4.3 Risk of Loss and Damage

Vendor bears the risk of loss, theft, or damage to the Device from the time of delivery. If the Device is lost, stolen, or damaged beyond normal wear and tear, Vendor agrees to pay Company a replacement fee of $        , which represents Company's reasonable cost to replace the Device.

4.4 Return of Device

Upon termination of this Agreement for any reason, Vendor must return the Device to Company in good working condition (normal wear and tear excepted) within 10 calendar days of the termination date. Company may remotely wipe the Device upon termination. Failure to return the Device within 10 days entitles Company to charge Vendor the replacement fee described in Section 4.3.

4.5 Device Condition at Handoff

Company and Vendor will document the Device's condition at the time of delivery (e.g., photographs, a signed delivery receipt). This record will serve as the baseline for any damage claims.

5. Founding Period — No-Cost Access

During the Founding Period (Agreement Date through September 30, 2026 inclusive), Company will provide Vendor with access to the Platform and the loaned Device at no charge. Vendor is not required to provide payment information during this period; however, see Section 6 regarding the mandatory pre-authorization acknowledgment below.

6. Conversion to Paid Subscription

6.1 Automatic Conversion

Beginning on October 1, 2026, Vendor's access to the Platform will automatically convert to a paid monthly subscription at the Founding Monthly Fee set forth in the Key Terms above, unless Vendor terminates this Agreement before that date in accordance with Section 12.

6.2 Pre-Billing Notice

Company will send Vendor written notice of the upcoming billing conversion at least 30 days before the first charge (no later than September 1, 2026) to the contact email address on file. The notice will state the Founding Monthly Fee, the billing start date, and instructions for cancellation if Vendor does not wish to continue.

6.3 Payment Authorization

Company will request valid payment method information (credit card, debit card, or ACH bank authorization) at the time of signing. If it is not provided then, Vendor must provide it no later than September 15, 2026, and Company will send payment setup instructions to the Vendor contact email no later than August 31, 2026. By providing payment information, Vendor authorizes Company to charge the Founding Monthly Fee to that payment method on a recurring monthly basis beginning October 1, 2026 until the Agreement is terminated.

Failure to provide payment information by September 15, 2026 does not suspend Vendor's payment obligation; any unpaid amounts will accrue from October 1, 2026 and Company may pursue collection through the dispute resolution process in Section 16.

6.4 Founding Rate Lock

As a founding vendor, your monthly fee is locked at the Founding Monthly Fee for as long as this Agreement remains in effect, provided your account is in good standing. This rate will not increase unless:

  • Company provides at least 60 days' written notice of a rate change; and
  • The new rate does not exceed the then-current Standard Rate charged to new vendors.

If Company raises the Founding Monthly Fee, Vendor may terminate this Agreement without penalty within the 60-day notice period.

6.5 Billing Cycle

Subscription fees are billed monthly, in advance, beginning October 1, 2026. Payment is due within 15 days of the invoice date. Accounts more than 30 days past due may be suspended until payment is received.

7. Vendor Obligations

  • Accurate Transactions: Vendor agrees to enter accurate dollar amounts when awarding points to students. Vendor is responsible for any points fraudulently or erroneously awarded through the terminal on Vendor's account.
  • PIN Security: Vendor must set a terminal PIN before accepting any point redemptions. Redeem and settings features are gated by this PIN, and until one is set they are not protected. Vendor is responsible for maintaining the confidentiality of the PIN, for limiting it to authorized staff, and for changing it immediately if Vendor believes it has been compromised.
  • Reward Fulfillment: Vendor agrees to honor all active rewards listed on its terminal. If Vendor cannot fulfill a reward, it should deactivate that reward through the ITEMS interface before accepting redemption codes for it.
  • Compliance with Law: Vendor agrees to operate the WeRewards program in compliance with all applicable local, state, and federal laws, including consumer protection and food service regulations.
  • Authorized Use Only: Vendor agrees not to use the Platform to defraud students, award false points, or manipulate the loyalty program in a manner inconsistent with genuine commercial transactions.
  • Internet Connectivity: Vendor is responsible for providing and maintaining internet service at its location sufficient to operate the terminal. Company is not responsible for downtime caused by connectivity issues at Vendor's location.
  • Notification of Changes: Vendor agrees to promptly notify Company of any change to the business's name, address, or contact information.

8. Company Obligations

  • Provide access to the Platform in substantial accordance with its current functionality;
  • Make reasonable efforts to maintain Platform availability, but without guaranteeing 100% uptime;
  • Provide reasonable support for Platform issues via email;
  • Provide Vendor with access to its transaction data and analytics through the terminal's STATS interface;
  • Deliver the 30-day billing notice as described in Section 6.2.

Company does not guarantee any particular number of student users, transaction volume, or revenue outcome for Vendor.

9. Platform Rules and Limits

  • The maximum single earn transaction is $200 (enforced by the Platform).
  • Void/reversal of a transaction is only available within 1 minute of the original transaction.
  • Vendor may set its own points_per_dollar rate within Platform-enforced limits (0.5 – 1,000 points per dollar).
  • Company may implement additional limits or platform rules from time to time and will notify Vendor of material changes.

10. Data and Privacy

10.1 Student Data

Vendor acknowledges that WeRewards collects personal information from students as described in the WeRewards Student Privacy Policy. Vendor agrees not to request, record, or use student personal data beyond what is displayed on the terminal during a legitimate transaction.

10.2 Vendor Business Data

Company retains all transaction records processed through the Platform. Vendor's business analytics (revenue, transaction counts, customer engagement data) are accessible to Vendor through the terminal's STATS interface. Company may use aggregated, anonymized transaction data across the Platform for analytics, reporting, or platform improvement purposes.

10.3 Vendor Application Data

Information submitted through the vendor application form is used solely to set up and manage Vendor's account. Company will not sell Vendor's contact information to third parties.

10.4 Data on Termination

Upon hard-deletion of a vendor account by Company, transaction records associated with the Vendor are anonymized (vendor identifier removed) but not deleted, to preserve historical integrity. Vendor's profile, staff, rewards, and session data are permanently deleted.

12. Termination

12.1 Termination by Vendor

Vendor may terminate this Agreement at any time by providing written notice to Company at contactwerewards@gmail.com. If Vendor terminates during the Founding Period, no fees are owed. If Vendor terminates after October 1, 2026, Vendor is responsible for any subscription fees accrued through the end of the current billing month. No refunds are issued for partial months.

12.2 Termination by Company

Company may terminate this Agreement immediately, without penalty, if Vendor:

  • Materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure the breach within 10 days of written notice;
  • Engages in fraud, abuse of the Platform, or conduct harmful to students or Company's reputation;
  • Fails to pay any subscription fee within 45 days of the due date.

Company may also terminate this Agreement without cause by providing 30 days' written notice to Vendor.

12.3 Effect of Termination

Upon termination, Vendor's access to the Platform is revoked, the Terminal Device must be returned per Section 4.4, and any students' unredeemed points associated with the Vendor will no longer be redeemable. Company is not liable for any losses Vendor or its customers sustain as a result of termination.

12.4 Suspension

Company may temporarily suspend Vendor's Platform access (without terminating this Agreement) for investigation of suspected fraud, abuse, or non-payment. Company will notify Vendor of the suspension and its reasons as promptly as reasonably possible.
